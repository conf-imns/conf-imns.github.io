2026 International Conference on Intelligent Multimedia, Networking, and Security (IMNS 2026)

Atlanta, Georgia, USA   |   August 3 - 4, 2026

Call for Papers

The IMNS 2026 is a global forum that brings together researchers, academics, and industry experts to exchange knowledge and advancements in multimedia technologies, communication networks, and cybersecurity. Technically Co-Sponsored by the IEEE and IEEE Communication Society, the event fosters interdisciplinary collaboration to promote the latest trends and practical implementations.

Scope and Topics

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

Intelligent Multimedia Systems

  • Content analysis, retrieval, and recommendation
  • Deep learning for image, video, and audio processing
  • AR/VR, metaverse, and immersive technologies
  • Quality of Experience (QoE) and Quality of Service (QoS)

Networking and Communications

  • Next-generation networks (5G/6G)
  • Edge, fog, and cloud computing
  • Software-Defined Networking (SDN) and NFV
  • Network slicing and virtualization

Security, Privacy, and Trust

  • Security for intelligent multimedia systems
  • Network security and intrusion detection
  • Applied cryptography and data protection
  • Trust management and privacy-preserving protocols

Intelligent and Autonomous Systems

  • AI-driven network management and optimization
  • Federated learning and distributed intelligence
  • Intelligent sensing and IoT applications

Submission Guidelines

Submission System: https://edas.info/newPaper.php?c=34698

IEEE Policy & Requirements

  • Originality: Submissions must be original, free of plagiarism, and not under review elsewhere.
  • Presentation: IEEE reserves the right to remove any paper from IEEE Xplore® if it is not presented at the conference.
  • Registration: At least one author must register at the FULL or LIMITED rate and present the paper. One registration covers up to two accepted papers.

Formatting & Submission

  • Initial Submission: PDF only via EDAS. Max 6 pages (10-point font). Papers exceeding 6 pages will be rejected without review.
  • Final Submission: Max 6 pages; up to 2 extra pages allowed at $100 per page.
  • Author Info: Author list and title must exactly match EDAS registration. Changes after acceptance are not allowed.
